[Writing] Population Aging

417字 2020-11-03
3 min read
Scroll Down


Currently, demographic change is poised to become one of the most significant global issues. Since the trajectory we are on is reliant on the fast development of science and technology, the better public health care system has made a lot of contributions to the improvement of life expectancy. However, declining fertility and increasing longevity have led to an unprecedented pace of population aging. Personally speaking, for society, I think problems of demographic change far outweigh its positive impact.

Firstly, considering the elderly are prone to have health problems, the government has to increase expenditure on medical services to treat age-related conditions, cut the budget in other essential services, and tax more to make ends meet. Therefore, from the perspective of economic fairness, it will hurt other tax-payers' rights to enjoy better infrastructure facilities, educational resources, and career opportunities. What's worsE, the rising tax burden and stressful life will affect people's livelihood and their working enthusiasm, which will catalyze threats to long-term economic growth in society.

Secondly, since the government has raised the pension eligibility age to keep the pension scheme running, senior citizens are likely to stay in the workforce even in their 60s. On the one hand, struggling with cognitive decline and other conditions, these elderly workers' ability to process information and handle work is limited. On the other hand, it will be difficult for the young generation to find job vacancies or get promoted. Therefore, from the perspective of economic efficiency, the postponement of retirement will exacerbate the unequal resource distribution and shrink the productivity and profitability of the whole society.

However, even if the increasing proportion of older people do seem to invoke some economic problems, some pros can still be under consideration from the opposite angle. The main benefit of these old workers is that they possess a plethora of practical experience, specialist knowledge, and work-related tips, which is an invisible asset to the whole society. By tapping into these predecessors' wisdom, young staff can minimize the risk of making mistakes and master working norms more efficiently. For instance, senior business managers will impart client management skills for dealing with complaints and queries to their juniors. Likewise, veteran scientists will pass on some methodology of overseeing and executing research or projects.

In conclusion, a rapidly aging population negatively affects economic fairness and efficiency. These social issues are difficult to tackle, although these experienced people can yield benefits to society by transferring their explicit knowledge and tacit knowledge.
